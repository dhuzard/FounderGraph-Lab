# Grant Memo - Innovate UK Digital Health Catalyst

## Thesis

TrialMesh can position itself as infrastructure for trusted, auditable clinical operations knowledge rather than as a generic AI summarization tool.

## Why the fit is plausible

- supports operational efficiency in regulated health settings
- improves traceability and evidence quality across distributed teams
- local-first approach may reduce governance friction
- could support faster startup for studies in oncology and rare disease

## Risks for grant fit

- benefits need stronger quantified public-interest framing
- product language currently sounds more sponsor-ops than digital health infrastructure
- UK partner strategy is not yet concrete

## Suggested positioning

Frame TrialMesh as a trusted evidence layer for trial startup readiness, helping teams coordinate faster without weakening governance.

## Next steps

- identify one UK oncology network partner
- quantify operational burden from fragmented startup evidence
- tie product to measurable readiness and documentation quality outcomes
