**Tagline:** BioVerify makes hospital lab QC instant. Reagent traceability for every clinical assay, in under 30 seconds.

## The problem

Hospital labs spend an estimated **18 hours per technician per week** on manual reagent-lot validation, calibration logs, and QC sign-offs. Failed audits cost a typical 300-bed hospital between $80K and $400K per incident, and CAP/CLIA inspections are getting stricter every cycle.

## What we sell

BioVerify is a barcode-and-camera workflow: a tablet at every bench scans every reagent and instrument, our cloud cross-checks the lot against the manufacturer's QC release certificate in real time, and the audit trail writes straight to the LIMS.

## Market size

Our **total addressable market is $50M annually** — that's the entire US clinical lab reagent QC software spend, based on a 2024 IBISWorld report. We expect to capture 20% in three years.

Later in the same deck, when discussing growth potential for the Series B:

> Globally, **the TAM expands to $5B** once you include all in-vitro diagnostic QC workflows, animal-health labs, contract research, and emerging-market hospital networks. Our wedge into US hospitals positions us perfectly to expand into this $5B opportunity by 2028.

## Pricing and unit economics

Our core assumption is that **hospitals will pay $10,000 per year per site** for the BioVerify platform, with implementation fees of $25K on top. At 5,000 US hospitals, that is a $50M ARR ceiling on the US lab segment alone.

We've validated this pricing thesis with three customer-discovery interviews (see appendix C). Hospitals universally rank reagent QC as a top-five compliance pain point.

## Traction

## Competitive landscape

- **LabWare** and **Beckman Remisol** offer LIMS-adjacent QC modules, but require IT integration projects measured in months.
- **No competitor** offers the camera-based bench workflow we do; we hold a provisional patent on the multi-lot vision pipeline.

## Risks

- **Reimbursement uncertainty.** Hospitals fund compliance tooling from operations budgets, which are flat YoY in 2025.
- **EHR integration depth.** Epic and Cerner gate third-party LIMS writes; we have a workaround via the Mirth connector layer, but it adds latency.
