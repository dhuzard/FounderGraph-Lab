# Customer Segments and Core Problem

## Segment 1: Biotech sponsor clinical operations teams

These teams own startup timelines but depend on medical writing, legal, finance, clinical science, data management, site activation, and vendors. They often operate with lean staff and fragmented systems.

Pain points:

- no single view of startup readiness
- protocol amendment impact is hard to trace
- evidence lives across email, SharePoint, Excel, and vendor portals
- startup reviews turn into meetings about document hunting rather than decisions

## Segment 2: Specialty CRO startup directors

These teams coordinate country activation, site qualification, training evidence, and vendor readiness. They are penalized when sponsors perceive startup delays.

Pain points:

- repeated requests for status updates without proof attached
- duplicated data entry between trackers and sponsor portals
- difficulty surfacing which blockers are sponsor-owned versus CRO-owned

## Segment 3: Academic medical center trial offices

These teams handle overlapping investigator-initiated and sponsored studies with local policy constraints.

Pain points:

- local policy documents and SOP evidence are scattered
- different departments maintain different naming conventions
- startup decisions depend on tribal knowledge from experienced coordinators

## Jobs to be done

- show which milestones are truly blocked
- prove that training, contracting, and site documents are in place
- identify unsupported assumptions before steering meetings
- prepare investor or grant narratives with concrete evidence

## Why now

The increase in cross-functional outsourcing and remote trial operations has expanded the volume of handoffs and evidence requests. Teams have more dashboards but less trust in their underlying document state.
