# TrialMesh Company Profile

## Summary

TrialMesh is a workflow intelligence startup for clinical trial startup teams at biotech sponsors, specialty CROs, and academic medical centers.

The platform ingests startup artifacts such as site feasibility questionnaires, protocol drafts, vendor trackers, contracting checklists, monitoring notes, and training evidence. TrialMesh organizes them into a structured evidence graph so teams can answer questions like:

- Which milestones are blocked by missing documents?
- Which assumptions about site readiness are unsupported?
- Which risks threaten first-patient-in timelines?
- Which vendors are involved in critical handoffs?

## Product

Core capabilities:

- document ingestion and normalization
- evidence graph of startup entities, relationships, and provenance
- deviation and dependency tracking
- site startup milestone map
- audit-ready evidence exports
- retrieval over vendor and startup notes

## Customer segments

- Series A to pre-IPO biotech sponsors with 3-20 active trials
- specialty CROs managing startup across multiple countries
- academic trial offices coordinating complex oncology and rare disease studies

## Problem

Clinical startup teams still coordinate critical work across email threads, shared drives, spreadsheet trackers, and fragmented vendor portals. Startup managers lose time reconciling protocol versions, collecting training proof, and proving readiness to internal and external stakeholders.

## Value proposition

TrialMesh reduces startup friction by making evidence traceable, searchable, and reviewable before decisions are escalated.

## Current assumptions

- Sponsors will pay for startup coordination if time-to-greenlight can be reduced by at least 15 percent.
- CRO program directors care more about auditability and reuse than about generic AI summarization.
- Medical centers will adopt a local-first deployment model if security review effort stays below 6 weeks.
